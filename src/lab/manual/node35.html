Second Method

Figure 5.3: Ziegler-Nichols instability tuning method

The second method is also known as `instability method '[2]. This is a closed loop method in which the integral and derivative gains of the PID controller are made zero with a unity value for proportional gain. A setpoint change is made and the temperature profile is observed for some time. The temperature would most likely maintain a steady-state with some offset. The gain is increased to a next distinct value (say 2) with a change in the setpoint. The procedure is repeated until the temperature first varies with sustained oscillations. It is necessary that the output (temperature) should have neither under damped nor over damped oscillations.
